# Larger East Prussia winter 1945 scenario maps

These are intentionally **larger operational-tactical maps** than the earlier versions. The idea is that the board should better match the scale implied by individual squads, vehicles, and guns, so that a scenario can last many turns and support factories, attrition, and maneuver.

## 1. `balga_heiligenbeil_corridor_1945_large.map`
This is a **zoomed-in coastal corridor scenario** inspired by the fighting west and southwest of Heiligenbeil, toward the Frisches Haff / Vistula Lagoon coast, during the formation of the Heiligenbeil Pocket in late January and February 1945. German 4th Army elements were compressed toward the lagoon coast and fought to keep evacuation corridors and coastal settlements open while Soviet forces pushed them back. The scenario therefore focuses on a narrower slice of the coastal retreat corridor rather than the whole pocket. The coastal water strip stands in for the lagoon/coastal edge, while multiple built-up nodes represent the chain of settlements, depots, and improvised strongpoints that mattered tactically. ## 2. `allenstein_lakes_approaches_1945_large.map`
This is a **larger inland battle-space abstraction** of the Allenstein (Olsztyn) sector during the Soviet January 1945 drive through southern East Prussia. Rather than depicting the entire breakthrough to the coast, it focuses on the tactically meaningful lake-and-town approach terrain around Allenstein: lakes, wooded ground, broken movement corridors, and urban nodes. Allenstein fell during Rokossovsky’s thrust that helped cut off German forces in East Prussia from the west, so this scenario is designed as a maneuver-and-node-control map rather than a simple frontal clash.
