# East Prussia winter 1945 scenario maps

## 1. `heiligenbeil_pocket_1945.map`
A compact gameplay abstraction of the **Heiligenbeil Pocket / Frisches Haff coastal fighting** in late January–March 1945.

What it is trying to represent:
- the German 4th Army compressed against the **Frisches Haff (Vistula Lagoon)** coast
- coastal evacuation pressure toward **Rosenberg / Balga / the lagoon shoreline**
- a chain of towns, road nodes, and improvised defensive positions
- Soviet pressure from the west and southwest through snowy open ground and woodland

How the terrain was translated into the game:
- the eastern water strip stands in for the **lagoon / coastal edge**
- the city/factory/HQ belt on the eastern side stands in for the shrinking German-held coastal settlements and evacuation corridor
- plains and forest inland represent the open winter approaches and wooded East Prussian countryside
- overlays such as trench, rubble, crater, and barbed wire represent prepared field positions and battle damage

This is not a literal hex-by-hex reconstruction. It is a **historically inspired scenario map** meant to feel like the late-winter East Prussian coastal pocket battles.

## 2. `allenstein_breakthrough_1945.map`
A compact gameplay abstraction of the **Allenstein (Olsztyn) sector** during the Soviet breakthrough in January 1945.

What it is trying to represent:
- the Soviet drive **through Allenstein toward the Gulf of Danzig**
- the inland East Prussian mix of **towns, lakes, forest, and winter roads**
- a battle space where urban nodes and narrow passable corridors matter more than a wide open tank plain

How the terrain was translated into the game:
- the central water clusters represent the **lake-heavy terrain** of southern East Prussia
- the city/factory cluster represents **Allenstein and nearby built-up transport/industrial nodes**
- forest and ridge tiles represent wooded ground and rougher defensive terrain
- overlays represent defensive fieldworks, damaged routes, and churned-up winter battlefield surfaces

Again, this is a **playable historical abstraction**, not a literal town plan.

## Suggested default scenario
If only one map is wired by default, I recommend using **`heiligenbeil_pocket_1945.map`** first because:
- it visually reads very clearly
- it uses the water edge well
- it makes the German defender / Soviet attacker setup immediately understandable
- it feels strongly “East Prussia winter 1945”
